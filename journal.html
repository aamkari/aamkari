THE JOURNAL

Stories Behind Every Thread

Every AAMKARI garment begins with a human story. Meet the artisans whose hands preserve centuries of Indian craftsmanship.

CHAPTER 01

Abdul Rahim Khatri

Master Bagh Printer

For over three decades, Abdul Rahim has continued one of India's oldest block-printing traditions from Bagh, Madhya Pradesh. Every wooden block is carved by hand. Every colour comes from natural minerals and plants. Every print carries generations of inherited knowledge. His work reminds us that true luxury is created patiently—never hurried.

CHAPTER 02

The Chanderi Weaver

Keeper of the Loom

Inside small weaving homes of Chanderi, every thread passes through skilled hands before becoming fabric. A single saree or abaya may require several days of uninterrupted weaving, balancing transparency, strength and elegance. The loom does not simply create cloth— it preserves history.

CHAPTER 03

The Needle Artist

Hand Embroidery Artisan

Every stitch reflects patience. Hand embroidery cannot be rushed. Each motif grows slowly, guided only by experience and memory. These artisans transform plain fabric into timeless heirlooms, ensuring every AAMKARI garment carries a personal human touch.
